Kickflip — Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 10, 2026 · App: Kickflip (com.kickflip.kickflip) · Developer contact: anguijm@gmail.com

Kickflip is an arcade skateboarding game. It was built to collect as little data as possible. There are no user accounts, no advertising, no analytics, and no sale or sharing of data for marketing of any kind. The entire game is playable offline; only the optional worldwide leaderboard uses the network.

Information the app handles

  • Anonymous identifier. If you submit a score to the leaderboard, the app signs in with Firebase Anonymous Authentication, which generates a random identifier for your device. It is not linked to your name, email address, phone number, or any other identity.
  • Skater name + score. The nickname you set and your best combo score are stored on the leaderboard in Google Cloud Firestore so scores can be ranked. The nickname can be anything; it is never verified or linked to a real identity.
  • On-device data. Your settings, records, unlocked cosmetics, and progress are stored only on your device and never uploaded.

What the app does NOT do

  • No advertising and no advertising SDKs.
  • No analytics or tracking SDKs.
  • No collection of contacts, location, photos, microphone, or files.
  • No sale, rental, or sharing of data with third parties for their own purposes.

Service providers

The optional leaderboard runs on Google Firebase (Firebase Authentication and Cloud Firestore), acting as a data processor that stores the nickname and score described above on the developer's behalf. Data is encrypted in transit (TLS). See Firebase's privacy documentation.

Data retention and deletion

You can stop using the app at any time; on-device data is removed by uninstalling the app or clearing its storage. To request deletion of a leaderboard entry associated with your device, email anguijm@gmail.com and it will be deleted.

Children

Kickflip is not directed at children under 13, and the app collects no personal information beyond what is described above.
